Chishimba Falls

Sacred waters of Northern Zambia

Three waterfalls on one river, each with its own character. A sacred cave behind the main cascade where no angry words may be spoken. A campsite that gives you the falls to yourself at sunset. Chishimba is Northern Province's most accessible natural spectacle — and one of Zambia's most beautiful.

The Falls

The Luombe River drops through three distinct falls within a kilometre of walking trail. Each has its own personality.

Mutumuna (upper falls, 20m) is a wide curtain of water — the broadest of the three. The Bemba believe the spirit Mutumuna resides here; the High Priest of Mutumuna still makes offerings at the falls. A hydroelectric weir sits just upstream, but the falls retain their power.

Kaela (middle rapids) is not a single plunge but a stepped cascade spreading across the river's width. The campsite sits on the eastern bank just above Kaela, offering what may be Zambia's best waterfall sunset: palm silhouettes against orange sky, the sound of rushing water as you cook dinner.

Chishimba (main falls, 30m) is the dramatic climax — a powerful plunge into a narrow gorge. Behind the curtain of water lies a cave sacred to the Bemba people, dwelling place of the spirit Chishimba. Visitors can walk behind the falls to enter this cave, but tradition demands respect: no insults, curses, or words of vengeance may be uttered here.

The trail connecting all three is well-maintained with viewing gazebos at key points. Allow two to three hours to see everything without rushing. The full walk involves some steps and uneven terrain, but nothing requiring special fitness.

The Power Station

The Chishimba hydroelectric power station has supplied electricity to Kasama since 1959. Infrastructure is visible above Mutumuna, but the falls themselves remain impressive. The station is currently being upgraded to 15MW capacity — a testament to the enduring power of these waters.

Getting There

From Kasama, take the Luwingu Road west. After 25km, turn right onto the gravel road signposted for Mporokoso. The falls are signposted 10km down this road. Total journey approximately 30–40 minutes from Kasama town.

By minibus: Vehicles heading to Mporokoso can drop you at the turn-off. It's about 1km walk from the junction to the car park.

Safety

  • Rocks near the falls are slippery — take care, especially after rain
  • The climb down to the base of Chishimba Falls involves about 60 metres of steps
  • Swimming is not recommended due to currents and sacred status
  • Respect the spiritual significance — follow local guidance at the cave

Tips for Visitors

  • Camp here: The sunset over Kaela is worth staying for
  • Combine with Mwela: The rock art site is 25km east of Kasama — an easy day trip pairing
  • Morning light: Best photography at the main falls is mid-morning when rainbows form in the spray
  • Bring cash: For entry fee and any camping charges

Suggested Itinerary

Kasama → Mwela Rocks (morning) → Kasama lunch → Chishimba Falls (afternoon/overnight) → continue to Mporokoso/Lumangwe
