Kifubwa Rock Shelter

Ancient engravings of North-Western Zambia

Prehistoric Rock Engravings

Kifubwa Rock Shelter is a significant archaeological site located in North-Western Province, featuring ancient rock engravings that provide a window into the lives and beliefs of Zambia's prehistoric inhabitants. Unlike the painted rock art found elsewhere in the country, Kifubwa is renowned for its carved petroglyphs - images etched directly into the rock surface.

The engravings at Kifubwa include geometric patterns, concentric circles, and abstract designs whose meanings continue to intrigue archaeologists and visitors alike. These images were created by carefully pecking and grinding the rock surface, a technique that required considerable skill and patience.

The rock shelter itself would have provided valuable protection from the elements for ancient peoples, and the engravings may have held spiritual or ceremonial significance. The site offers important evidence of early human habitation in northwestern Zambia and connects to broader traditions of rock art found across southern and central Africa.

Visiting the Site

Kifubwa Rock Shelter is located near Solwezi in North-Western Province. The site is accessible by road and visitors can view the engravings up close. The best time to see the petroglyphs is when the sun is at an angle that creates shadows in the carved lines, making them more visible. Local guides can provide context about the site's history and significance.
