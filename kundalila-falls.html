Kundalila Falls

The Crying Dove of Central Zambia

The Crying Dove

Kundalila Falls is one of Zambia's most breathtaking natural wonders, a stunning 75-meter waterfall whose name means "Crying Dove" in the Bemba language. Located near Serenje in Central Province, the falls cascade dramatically into a crystal-clear pool surrounded by pristine miombo woodland.

The waterfall gets its poetic name from the sound the water makes as it plunges over the cliff edge - a gentle, mournful cry that echoes through the surrounding forest. The spray from the falls creates a permanent mist that nurtures lush vegetation on the surrounding rocks, creating a verdant oasis in the dry season.

The pool at the base of the falls is safe for swimming during the dry season, offering visitors a refreshing reward after the hike down. The surrounding area is home to diverse birdlife and the occasional monkey troop, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

Visiting the Falls

Kundalila Falls is located about 55 kilometers from Serenje, off the Great North Road. The site has basic facilities and a well-maintained trail leading to various viewpoints and down to the pool. The falls are most impressive during and just after the rainy season (November to April), though swimming is best in the dry season when water levels are lower.
