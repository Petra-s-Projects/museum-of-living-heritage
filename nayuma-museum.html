Nayuma Museum

Preserving the heritage of Barotseland

Gateway to Lozi Culture

Nayuma Museum, located in Mongu in Western Province, is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Lozi people and the historic Barotseland kingdom. The museum offers visitors a comprehensive introduction to one of Zambia's most distinctive and well-preserved traditional cultures.

The museum's collections include traditional artifacts, royal regalia, historical photographs, and displays explaining the customs, ceremonies, and way of life of the Lozi people. Highlights include items related to the Kuomboka ceremony - the famous annual migration of the Litunga (king) from the floodplains to higher ground - one of Africa's most spectacular traditional events.

Visitors can learn about the unique political system of Barotseland, the traditional crafts of the region including basket weaving and woodcarving, and the spiritual beliefs that have shaped Lozi society for centuries. The museum serves as an important educational resource for both Zambians and international visitors.

Visiting the Museum

Nayuma Museum is located in Mongu, the capital of Western Province. The museum is open to visitors throughout the year and offers guided tours that provide deeper insights into Lozi culture. A visit to the museum pairs well with exploring other cultural sites in the area, including the Litunga's palace and the Kuomboka harbor.
