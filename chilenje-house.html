Chilenje House 394

Birthplace of Zambian independence

The Independence Home

Chilenje House 394 stands as one of Zambia's most significant historic buildings - the home where Kenneth Kaunda, Zambia's founding father and first President, lived during the crucial years of the independence struggle. This modest house in Lusaka's Chilenje township became a nerve center for the independence movement.

It was here that key meetings were held, strategies were planned, and the vision for a free Zambia took shape. The house witnessed the formation of political alliances and the organization of peaceful resistance against colonial rule. Kaunda lived here with his family while leading the United National Independence Party (UNIP) toward the historic moment of independence on October 24, 1964.

Today, Chilenje House 394 has been preserved as a national monument and museum, offering visitors a glimpse into the humble origins of Zambia's liberation movement. The house contains original furnishings, photographs, and memorabilia from the independence era.

Visiting the House

Chilenje House 394 is located in Chilenje South township, Lusaka. The museum is open to visitors and provides guided tours that bring to life the story of Zambia's independence struggle. The site offers an intimate understanding of the personal sacrifices made by those who fought for freedom.
